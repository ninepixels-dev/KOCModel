This BRA Phase reviews the building risks during the Project Execution (Phase 1): Detailed Engineering & Design (PGS 4.1).

Phase 3 of BRA may only be required for unusual cases where risk-reduction measures are not clearly appropriate. In this case, a full-blown QRA is performed to evaluate the acceptability of the risks associated with the buildings of concern.

This phase of BRA reviews the risks associated with the identified hazards. It is anticipated that new projects should rarely require BRA Phase 3 because it is feasible to mitigate the hazards identified in previous BRA Phases by proper attention to building design and location.

If BRA Phase 3 is required, the KOC Controlling Team must review and approve the scope, methodology, and results of the analysis.

Details regarding BRA Phase 3 shall be determined on a case-by-case basis after the conclusion of BRA Phase 2.
