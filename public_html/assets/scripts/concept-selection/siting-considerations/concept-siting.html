Concept Siting Considerations


During the facility siting for concept selection the end user should consider the following siting considerations:

  • When selecting the site, an ideal choice would be one with no neighbours to minimize off-site risk potential. The decision should focus on the density of population surrounding the proposed site and existence of any sensitive populations or areas. A site in a remote, unpopulated area is preferable to a site in a highly populated urban area
  • Where the residential housing is located in proximity to a facility, the housing shall be upwind or crosswind of the prevailing wind direction
  • The prevailing wind direction applicable to a particular location shall be determined for each case. Appropriate consideration of the prevailing wind conditions is one of the most critical siting aspects during this stage of the project.
  • In selecting a site, allowance should be made for facility emergencies.
Further information in relation to these aspects is given in the Guidelines, Section 5.2.
