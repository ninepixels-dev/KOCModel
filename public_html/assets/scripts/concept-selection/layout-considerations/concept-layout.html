Concept Layout & Segregation Considerations


During the facility siting for concept selection the end user should consider the following layout and segregation considerations:

  • Using hazard assessments for each facility operation to assist with establishing the layout or orientation of process unit battery limits within the proposed facility
  • Reviewing the possible accident events and the consequences for the proposed facility
  • Selecting a layout which is expected to reduce the overall likelihood of the property damage and associated business interruptions should an incident occur
  • Locating occupied buildings away from hazardous areas, such that the risk to occupants is eliminated or otherwise reduced to ALARP
  • Locating facility offices, workshops, laboratories and other similar buildings not directly linked to facility operation control separately from the control building in a non-hazardous area
  • Locating onsite facilities such that overhead electrical power transmission lines do not cross-over the site (for additional details on clearance from overhead power transmission lines refer to the relevant KOC document
  • Accessibility for maintenance and operations in determining spacing arrangements, as well as locating:
    • Equipment needing frequent overhaul, maintenance or cleaning at unit boundaries
    • Large vessels or equipment close to unit boundaries to allow easy access of cranes etc.

Further information in relation to these aspects is given in the Guidelines, Section 5.3.
