Concept Equipment Spacing Considerations


Concept spacing is related to achieving a reasonably safe layout aimed at decrease project and operational costs because appropriate separation between hazards typically decreases protection requirements. For example, a control room not exposed to significant explosion loads from a processing unit would have no need to be as structurally robust as would be the case if it were poorly located.

Adequate spacing between hazard areas reduces the overall major accident potential. The optimum layout should achieve a balance between loss prevention, construction, maintenance and operation requirements.

Further information in relation to these aspects is given in the Guidelines, Section 5.4.
